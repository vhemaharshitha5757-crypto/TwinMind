1. SUMMARY
========================================================================

AWS S3 bucket (s3-prod-assets-bucket) began returning AccessDenied errors for all read operations at 15:30 UTC on September 22, 2025. A bucket policy update intended to restrict public access inadvertently also removed the VPC endpoint policy that allowed application servers to read assets. All static asset loading (images, CSS, JS) failed across the platform.

This incident was classified as P2 severity and was assigned to
Lisa Park in the Cloud Infrastructure department. The affected system was
AWS S3, specifically the s3-prod-assets-bucket component located in US-East-1 (AWS).
The incident was detected through automated monitoring and alerting systems,
and the response team was engaged within minutes of the initial alert.

========================================================================
2. BUSINESS IMPACT
========================================================================

All static assets (images, stylesheets, JavaScript bundles) unavailable for 20 minutes. Website rendered without styling or images, appearing broken. 18,000 users experienced degraded visual experience. Mobile app asset loading failed. Revenue impact from degraded user experience estimated at $45,000.

The incident triggered the organization's major incident response process,
involving cross-functional coordination between engineering, operations, and
business stakeholders. Customer support was notified and prepared to handle
incoming queries related to the service degradation.

3. SYMPTOMS OBSERVED

The following symptoms were observed during the incident:

8. PREVENTIVE ACTIONS
========================================================================

The following preventive actions have been identified to reduce the
likelihood and impact of similar incidents in the future:

  1. Implement S3 bucket policy change simulation before applying
  2. Create bucket policy templates that include standard VPC endpoint access
  3. Add S3 access monitoring with immediate alerting on AccessDenied spikes
  4. Implement bucket policy change approval workflow
  5. Create pre-deployment checklist for S3 policy modifications

These action items will be tracked in the engineering backlog and prioritized
based on risk assessment and business impact analysis.
