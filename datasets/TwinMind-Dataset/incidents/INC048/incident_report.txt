1. SUMMARY
========================================================================

The Kubernetes Horizontal Pod Autoscaler (HPA) for the frontend service failed to scale up during a major traffic surge at 18:00 UTC on December 8, 2025. The metrics-server pod that HPA relies on for CPU metrics had been silently OOMKilled 2 hours earlier. Without metrics, HPA could not detect the increased load and kept the pod count at the minimum of 3, while traffic required at least 20 pods. The frontend service became overwhelmed.

This incident was classified as P1 severity and was assigned to
Alex Johnson in the Platform Engineering department. The affected system was
Google Kubernetes Engine, specifically the hpa-frontend-prod component located in GCP us-central1.
The incident was detected through automated monitoring and alerting systems,
and the response team was engaged within minutes of the initial alert.

========================================================================
2. BUSINESS IMPACT
========================================================================

Frontend service overwhelmed during peak traffic event (marketing campaign launch). Response times exceeded 15 seconds. 65% of requests timed out. Approximately 42,000 users impacted. Campaign launch was considered a failure. Estimated $280,000 in lost campaign revenue.

The incident triggered the organization's major incident response process,
involving cross-functional coordination between engineering, operations, and
business stakeholders. Customer support was notified and prepared to handle
incoming queries related to the service degradation.

7. RESOLUTION
========================================================================

The incident was resolved by the following actions: Restarted metrics-server with increased memory allocation, manually scaled frontend to 20 replicas while HPA recovered, and implemented metrics-server monitoring.

The resolution was executed by Alex Johnson with support from the incident
response team. After applying the remediation steps, the team monitored the
system for a stabilization period to confirm that the issue was fully
resolved and no secondary or cascading failures had occurred. All affected

8. PREVENTIVE ACTIONS
========================================================================

The following preventive actions have been identified to reduce the
likelihood and impact of similar incidents in the future:

  1. Implement metrics-server health monitoring with immediate alerting
  2. Increase metrics-server memory allocation with proper resource requests
  3. Add HPA fallback using custom metrics from Prometheus as secondary source
  4. Implement manual scaling alerts when HPA is unable to function
  5. Create pre-campaign scaling runbook with manual scale-up checklist

These action items will be tracked in the engineering backlog and prioritized
based on risk assessment and business impact analysis.
