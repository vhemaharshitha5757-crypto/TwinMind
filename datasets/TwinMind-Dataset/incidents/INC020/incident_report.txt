1. SUMMARY
========================================================================

The NFS mount point for shared application data (nfs-prod-share-01) became inaccessible on 12 application servers at 11:15 UTC on May 26, 2025. The NetApp storage controller experienced a failover to its partner node during routine firmware maintenance, but the NFS clients did not reconnect automatically due to hard mount configuration without timeout values. Application processes attempting to access the NFS share hung indefinitely.

This incident was classified as P2 severity and was assigned to
Chris Evans in the Storage Engineering department. The affected system was
NetApp NFS Cluster, specifically the nfs-prod-share-01 component located in On-Premises DC-Chicago.
The incident was detected through automated monitoring and alerting systems,
and the response team was engaged within minutes of the initial alert.

========================================================================
2. BUSINESS IMPACT
========================================================================

12 application servers became unresponsive with hung I/O operations for 35 minutes. User file upload and document processing features were unavailable. Approximately 2,400 users were affected. 850 file upload operations failed and required retry.

The incident triggered the organization's major incident response process,
involving cross-functional coordination between engineering, operations, and
business stakeholders. Customer support was notified and prepared to handle
incoming queries related to the service degradation.

========================================================================
3. SYMPTOMS OBSERVED
========================================================================

The following symptoms were observed during the incident:

  * NFS mount points showing stale file handle errors on application servers
  * Application processes in D (uninterruptible sleep) state waiting for NFS I/O
  * NetApp storage controller showing successful failover to partner node
  * NFS clients not reconnecting after storage controller failover
  * df and ls commands hanging indefinitely on affected mount points

These symptoms were correlated across multiple monitoring systems including
application performance monitoring, infrastructure metrics, and log
aggregation platforms. The incident response team confirmed the symptoms
during their initial assessment and used them to guide the investigation.

========================================================================
7. RESOLUTION
========================================================================

The incident was resolved by the following actions: Forced NFS unmount and remount with soft mount options on all affected servers, verified storage controller health, and updated NFS mount configuration to include reconnection parameters.

The resolution was executed by Chris Evans with support from the incident
response team. After applying the remediation steps, the team monitored the
system for a stabilization period to confirm that the issue was fully
resolved and no secondary or cascading failures had occurred. All affected
services were verified to be operating within normal parameters before the
incident was formally closed.

========================================================================
8. PREVENTIVE ACTIONS
========================================================================

The following preventive actions have been identified to reduce the
likelihood and impact of similar incidents in the future:

  1. Update NFS mount configuration to use soft mounts with timeout values
  2. Implement NFS mount health monitoring with automatic remount capability
  3. Coordinate storage controller maintenance with application team notifications
  4. Add NFS client reconnection testing to storage maintenance procedures
  5. Create automated NFS mount recovery script for failover scenarios

These action items will be tracked in the engineering backlog and prioritized
based on risk assessment and business impact analysis.
