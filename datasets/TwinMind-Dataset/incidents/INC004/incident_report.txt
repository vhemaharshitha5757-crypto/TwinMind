1. SUMMARY
========================================================================

Production application server app-prod-web-07 reached 100% disk utilization at 18:22 UTC on February 3, 2025. The disk filled due to verbose debug logging that was accidentally left enabled after a troubleshooting session three days earlier. The application could not write temporary files or log new entries, causing request processing failures. The server was behind a load balancer but health checks continued to pass, routing live traffic to the degraded node.

This incident was classified as P2 severity and was assigned to
Lisa Park in the Cloud Infrastructure department. The affected system was
EC2 Instance Fleet, specifically the app-prod-web-07 component located in US-East-1 (AWS).
The incident was detected through automated monitoring and alerting systems,
and the response team was engaged within minutes of the initial alert.

========================================================================
2. BUSINESS IMPACT
========================================================================

Approximately 12.5% of production web traffic was routed to the degraded server (1 of 8 nodes). Affected users experienced 500 Internal Server errors on all requests. An estimated 3,200 users were impacted over 22 minutes. No data loss occurred but customer satisfaction scores dropped during the incident window.

The incident triggered the organization's major incident response process,
involving cross-functional coordination between engineering, operations, and

7. RESOLUTION
========================================================================

The incident was resolved by the following actions: Cleared stale log files, disabled debug logging, resized the EBS volume from 200GB to 500GB, and updated health check to verify disk space availability.

The resolution was executed by Lisa Park with support from the incident
response team. After applying the remediation steps, the team monitored the
system for a stabilization period to confirm that the issue was fully
resolved and no secondary or cascading failures had occurred. All affected
services were verified to be operating within normal parameters before the
incident was formally closed.

========================================================================
8. PREVENTIVE ACTIONS
========================================================================

The following preventive actions have been identified to reduce the
likelihood and impact of similar incidents in the future:

  1. Implement automated log rotation with size-based limits
  2. Add disk utilization alerting at 80% and 90% thresholds
  3. Update health check endpoints to include disk space verification
  4. Create automated process to detect and disable debug logging in production
  5. Standardize EBS volume sizing with growth capacity planning

These action items will be tracked in the engineering backlog and prioritized
based on risk assessment and business impact analysis.
