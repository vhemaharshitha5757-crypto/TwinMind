1. SUMMARY
========================================================================

A critical batch processing VM (batch-processor-spot-12) was unexpectedly terminated at 16:45 UTC on March 31, 2025 due to AWS spot instance reclamation. The instance was running a 4-hour financial reconciliation batch job that was 3 hours into processing when terminated. The architecture incorrectly used spot instances for this long-running critical workload without checkpointing or fallback to on-demand instances.

This incident was classified as P2 severity and was assigned to
Lisa Park in the Cloud Infrastructure department. The affected system was
AWS EC2 Spot Instances, specifically the batch-processor-spot-12 component located in US-West-2 (AWS).
The incident was detected through automated monitoring and alerting systems,
and the response team was engaged within minutes of the initial alert.

2. BUSINESS IMPACT

Financial reconciliation batch job for Q1 closing was interrupted, requiring a complete restart. The 3 hours of processing were lost. The job completion was delayed by 7 hours total, causing the Q1 financial close to miss the internal deadline by one business day. Finance team required to work overtime.

========================================================================
8. PREVENTIVE ACTIONS
========================================================================

The following preventive actions have been identified to reduce the
likelihood and impact of similar incidents in the future:

  1. Migrate all critical batch workloads to on-demand or reserved instances
  2. Implement job checkpointing every 15 minutes for batch processing
  3. Add spot interruption handling with automatic migration to on-demand
  4. Create workload classification policy for spot vs on-demand usage
  5. Implement batch job progress monitoring with alerts for stalled jobs

These action items will be tracked in the engineering backlog and prioritized
based on risk assessment and business impact analysis.
