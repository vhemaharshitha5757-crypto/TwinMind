1. SUMMARY
========================================================================

The GCP Cloud Load Balancer (glb-prod-frontend) began removing healthy backend instances from its serving pool at 07:30 UTC on April 28, 2025. A health check configuration change reduced the check interval from 10 seconds to 1 second and the healthy threshold from 2 to 1, making the health checks overly sensitive. Normal application response time jitter caused intermittent health check failures, leading to instances being cycled in and out of the pool.

This incident was classified as P2 severity and was assigned to
Alex Johnson in the SRE department. The affected system was
GCP Cloud Load Balancer, specifically the glb-prod-frontend component located in GCP us-central1.
The incident was detected through automated monitoring and alerting systems,
and the response team was engaged within minutes of the initial alert.

========================================================================
2. BUSINESS IMPACT
========================================================================

Backend capacity fluctuated between 20% and 100% over 25 minutes, causing intermittent 503 errors during low-capacity windows. Approximately 3,800 users experienced sporadic errors. Auto-scaler attempted to compensate by launching new instances, increasing cloud costs by $2,400.

The incident triggered the organization's major incident response process,
involving cross-functional coordination between engineering, operations, and
business stakeholders. Customer support was notified and prepared to handle
incoming queries related to the service degradation.

3. SYMPTOMS OBSERVED

The following symptoms were observed during the incident:

  * Backend instances being removed and re-added to load balancer every 5-15 seconds
  * Intermittent 503 Service Unavailable errors during capacity dips
  * Health check logs showing rapid HEALTHY/UNHEALTHY state transitions
  * Auto-scaler launching excessive instances to compensate for perceived failures
  * Application response times normal when accessed directly

These symptoms were correlated across multiple monitoring systems including
application performance monitoring, infrastructure metrics, and log

========================================================================
6. ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
========================================================================

The root cause of this incident was determined to be:
Overly aggressive health check configuration with 1-second interval and single-check threshold causing false-positive failures from normal response time variance.

Further analysis confirmed that this issue had been developing over time but
was not detected by existing monitoring thresholds and alerting rules. The
failure point was within the glb-prod-frontend component of the GCP Cloud Load Balancer
system. Contributing factors included insufficient monitoring coverage for
the specific failure mode encountered and the absence of automated
remediation procedures for this particular scenario.

========================================================================
7. RESOLUTION
========================================================================

The incident was resolved by the following actions: Restored health check configuration to 10-second interval with 3 consecutive checks required, stabilized the backend pool, and terminated excess auto-scaled instances.

The resolution was executed by Alex Johnson with support from the incident
response team. After applying the remediation steps, the team monitored the
system for a stabilization period to confirm that the issue was fully
resolved and no secondary or cascading failures had occurred. All affected
services were verified to be operating within normal parameters before the
incident was formally closed.

The following preventive actions have been identified to reduce the
