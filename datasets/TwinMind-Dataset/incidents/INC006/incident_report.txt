1. SUMMARY
========================================================================

All pods in the payment namespace of the production GKE cluster entered CrashLoopBackOff state at 06:12 UTC on February 17, 2025. The issue was triggered by a Kubernetes secret rotation that updated the database credentials secret, but the pod deployment was referencing the old secret version. Pods could not authenticate to the database on startup and immediately crashed, with Kubernetes repeatedly attempting to restart them.

This incident was classified as P1 severity and was assigned to
Alex Johnson in the Platform Engineering department. The affected system was
Google Kubernetes Engine, specifically the payment-namespace-pods component located in GCP us-central1.
The incident was detected through automated monitoring and alerting systems,
and the response team was engaged within minutes of the initial alert.

========================================================================
2. BUSINESS IMPACT
========================================================================

Complete payment processing outage for 38 minutes. All credit card transactions, refunds, and payment webhooks failed during the window. Approximately 5,600 transactions were queued in the message broker. Revenue impact estimated at $210,000. PCI compliance incident report was required.

The incident triggered the organization's major incident response process,
involving cross-functional coordination between engineering, operations, and
business stakeholders. Customer support was notified and prepared to handle
incoming queries related to the service degradation.

6. ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
========================================================================

The root cause of this incident was determined to be:
Kubernetes secret rotation updated database credentials but pod deployments were pinned to the old secret version, causing authentication failures on every pod restart.

Further analysis confirmed that this issue had been developing over time but
was not detected by existing monitoring thresholds and alerting rules. The
failure point was within the payment-namespace-pods component of the Google Kubernetes Engine
system. Contributing factors included insufficient monitoring coverage for
the specific failure mode encountered and the absence of automated
remediation procedures for this particular scenario.

========================================================================
7. RESOLUTION
========================================================================

The incident was resolved by the following actions: Updated pod deployment manifests to reference the new secret version, performed rolling restart of all payment namespace pods, and drained the queued transactions.

The resolution was executed by Alex Johnson with support from the incident
response team. After applying the remediation steps, the team monitored the
system for a stabilization period to confirm that the issue was fully
resolved and no secondary or cascading failures had occurred. All affected
services were verified to be operating within normal parameters before the
incident was formally closed.

========================================================================
8. PREVENTIVE ACTIONS
========================================================================

The following preventive actions have been identified to reduce the
likelihood and impact of similar incidents in the future:

  1. Implement automatic secret version synchronization in deployments
  2. Add pre-rotation validation checks for secret consumers
  3. Create Kubernetes admission webhook to prevent secret version mismatches
  4. Implement graceful degradation for database connectivity in payment services
  5. Add payment namespace pod health monitoring with business-level alerts

These action items will be tracked in the engineering backlog and prioritized
based on risk assessment and business impact analysis.
