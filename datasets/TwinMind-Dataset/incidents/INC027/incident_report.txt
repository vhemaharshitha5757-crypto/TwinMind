1. SUMMARY
========================================================================

The corporate VPN gateway (vpn-gw-london-01) stopped accepting new connections at 07:00 UTC on July 14, 2025. The VPN concentrator's session table was full at 10,000 concurrent sessions due to a combination of orphaned sessions not being cleaned up and a 15% increase in remote workforce since the last capacity review. Existing sessions continued to work but no new connections could be established.

This incident was classified as P2 severity and was assigned to
Maria Garcia in the Networking department. The affected system was
Palo Alto GlobalProtect VPN, specifically the vpn-gw-london-01 component located in On-Premises DC-London.
The incident was detected through automated monitoring and alerting systems,
and the response team was engaged within minutes of the initial alert.

========================================================================
2. BUSINESS IMPACT
========================================================================

Approximately 2,200 remote employees in EMEA were unable to connect to the VPN for 45 minutes during morning login hours. Internal applications, email, and development environments were inaccessible. Productivity loss estimated at 1,650 person-hours. Several critical meetings were disrupted.

The incident triggered the organization's major incident response process,
involving cross-functional coordination between engineering, operations, and
business stakeholders. Customer support was notified and prepared to handle
incoming queries related to the service degradation.

6. ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
========================================================================

The root cause of this incident was determined to be:
VPN session table exhaustion due to orphaned sessions from improper disconnects accumulating over time combined with workforce growth exceeding planned capacity.

Further analysis confirmed that this issue had been developing over time but
was not detected by existing monitoring thresholds and alerting rules. The
failure point was within the vpn-gw-london-01 component of the Palo Alto GlobalProtect VPN
system. Contributing factors included insufficient monitoring coverage for
the specific failure mode encountered and the absence of automated
remediation procedures for this particular scenario.

========================================================================
7. RESOLUTION
========================================================================

The incident was resolved by the following actions: Cleared 3,200 orphaned VPN sessions, increased session table limit to 15,000, implemented aggressive session timeout for idle connections, and added a secondary VPN gateway for overflow.

The resolution was executed by Maria Garcia with support from the incident
response team. After applying the remediation steps, the team monitored the
system for a stabilization period to confirm that the issue was fully
resolved and no secondary or cascading failures had occurred. All affected
services were verified to be operating within normal parameters before the
incident was formally closed.

========================================================================
8. PREVENTIVE ACTIONS
========================================================================

The following preventive actions have been identified to reduce the
likelihood and impact of similar incidents in the future:

  1. Implement automated orphaned session cleanup every 4 hours
  2. Increase VPN capacity planning reviews to quarterly
  3. Deploy secondary VPN gateway for load distribution and redundancy
  4. Add VPN session utilization monitoring with alerts at 70% and 90%
  5. Implement VPN session analytics for capacity forecasting

These action items will be tracked in the engineering backlog and prioritized
based on risk assessment and business impact analysis.
