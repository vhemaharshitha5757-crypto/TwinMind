1. SUMMARY
========================================================================

LDAP authentication bind operations began failing on the London Active Directory domain controller (ad-dc-london-02) at 05:30 UTC on November 10, 2025, immediately following an overnight security patch. The patch updated the LDAP channel binding requirements from 'when supported' to 'always required', but legacy application LDAP clients did not support channel binding. All applications using simple LDAP bind against this DC were unable to authenticate users.

This incident was classified as P1 severity and was assigned to
Aisha Khan in the Identity Engineering department. The affected system was
Microsoft Active Directory, specifically the ad-dc-london-02 component located in On-Premises DC-London.
The incident was detected through automated monitoring and alerting systems,
and the response team was engaged within minutes of the initial alert.

========================================================================
2. BUSINESS IMPACT
========================================================================

All LDAP-dependent applications in EMEA unable to authenticate users for 40 minutes. VPN authentication, internal wiki, ticketing system, and 8 other internal applications affected. 3,100 EMEA employees unable to log in at start of business. Productivity loss estimated at 2,000 person-hours.

The incident triggered the organization's major incident response process,
involving cross-functional coordination between engineering, operations, and
business stakeholders. Customer support was notified and prepared to handle
incoming queries related to the service degradation.

3. SYMPTOMS OBSERVED

The following symptoms were observed during the incident:

  * LDAP bind operations returning 'Strong auth required' error (LDAP error 8)

  * Applications using Kerberos authentication unaffected

These symptoms were correlated across multiple monitoring systems including

7. RESOLUTION
========================================================================

The incident was resolved by the following actions: Temporarily reverted LDAP channel binding policy to 'when supported', scheduled application updates to support channel binding, and created migration plan for channel binding enforcement.

The resolution was executed by Aisha Khan with support from the incident
response team. After applying the remediation steps, the team monitored the
system for a stabilization period to confirm that the issue was fully
resolved and no secondary or cascading failures had occurred. All affected

8. PREVENTIVE ACTIONS
========================================================================

The following preventive actions have been identified to reduce the
likelihood and impact of similar incidents in the future:

  1. Inventory all applications using LDAP simple bind for migration planning
  2. Implement LDAP channel binding support in all application LDAP clients
  3. Create AD patch testing environment mirroring production LDAP usage
  4. Add pre-patch validation of LDAP binding compatibility
  5. Schedule phased LDAP channel binding enforcement with application readiness gates

These action items will be tracked in the engineering backlog and prioritized
based on risk assessment and business impact analysis.
