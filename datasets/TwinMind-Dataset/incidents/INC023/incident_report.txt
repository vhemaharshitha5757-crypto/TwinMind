1. SUMMARY
========================================================================

AWS CloudFront CDN distribution (cdn-dist-prod-01) continued serving stale product pricing and inventory data for 2 hours after a backend update at 08:20 UTC on June 16, 2025. The cache invalidation request submitted during deployment failed silently due to an expired IAM credential used by the deployment script. Users were seeing outdated prices and availability status for products that had been updated.

This incident was classified as P3 severity and was assigned to
Sarah Chen in the Platform Engineering department. The affected system was
AWS CloudFront CDN, specifically the cdn-dist-prod-01 component located in Global (CloudFront).
The incident was detected through automated monitoring and alerting systems,
and the response team was engaged within minutes of the initial alert.

========================================================================
2. BUSINESS IMPACT
========================================================================

Product pricing discrepancies for 2 hours across all regions. 340 orders were placed with outdated pricing, requiring manual price adjustment credits totaling $8,200. Customer confusion about product availability led to 120 support tickets.

The incident triggered the organization's major incident response process,
involving cross-functional coordination between engineering, operations, and
business stakeholders. Customer support was notified and prepared to handle
incoming queries related to the service degradation.

6. ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
========================================================================

The root cause of this incident was determined to be:
CDN cache invalidation failed silently during deployment because the IAM service account credentials used by the deployment script had expired.

Further analysis confirmed that this issue had been developing over time but
was not detected by existing monitoring thresholds and alerting rules. The
failure point was within the cdn-dist-prod-01 component of the AWS CloudFront CDN
system. Contributing factors included insufficient monitoring coverage for
the specific failure mode encountered and the absence of automated
remediation procedures for this particular scenario.

========================================================================
7. RESOLUTION
========================================================================

The incident was resolved by the following actions: Rotated IAM credentials, triggered manual CloudFront cache invalidation for all product-related paths, verified fresh content being served from all edge locations.

The resolution was executed by Sarah Chen with support from the incident
response team. After applying the remediation steps, the team monitored the
system for a stabilization period to confirm that the issue was fully
resolved and no secondary or cascading failures had occurred. All affected
services were verified to be operating within normal parameters before the
incident was formally closed.

========================================================================
8. PREVENTIVE ACTIONS
========================================================================

The following preventive actions have been identified to reduce the
likelihood and impact of similar incidents in the future:

  1. Implement IAM credential rotation monitoring with expiry alerts
  2. Add cache invalidation verification step in deployment pipeline
  3. Create automated cache freshness testing after deployments
  4. Implement cache-busting versioned URLs for dynamic content
  5. Add deployment pipeline gate requiring successful invalidation before completion

These action items will be tracked in the engineering backlog and prioritized
based on risk assessment and business impact analysis.
