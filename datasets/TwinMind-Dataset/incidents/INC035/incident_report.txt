1. SUMMARY
========================================================================

MySQL production database (mysql-prod-primary-01) experienced widespread lock wait timeout errors starting at 06:30 UTC on September 8, 2025. A long-running reporting query that was accidentally executed against the primary database (instead of the read replica) acquired a table-level lock that blocked all DML operations on the accounts table for 15 minutes. All application writes to the accounts table timed out.

This incident was classified as P3 severity and was assigned to
Rajesh Kumar in the Database Engineering department. The affected system was
MySQL Database, specifically the mysql-prod-primary-01 component located in US-West-2 (AWS).
The incident was detected through automated monitoring and alerting systems,
and the response team was engaged within minutes of the initial alert.

========================================================================
2. BUSINESS IMPACT
========================================================================

Account-related operations blocked for 15 minutes. User registration, profile updates, and account settings changes all failed. Approximately 800 users were unable to register or update their accounts. Support tickets generated for registration failures.

The incident triggered the organization's major incident response process,
involving cross-functional coordination between engineering, operations, and
business stakeholders. Customer support was notified and prepared to handle
incoming queries related to the service degradation.

The following symptoms were observed during the incident:

  * Only accounts table affected, other tables operating normally

These symptoms were correlated across multiple monitoring systems including

The root cause of this incident was determined to be:

Further analysis confirmed that this issue had been developing over time but

========================================================================
7. RESOLUTION
========================================================================

The incident was resolved by the following actions: Killed the blocking reporting query, waiting connections automatically retried, implemented query routing to prevent analytical queries from reaching the primary.

The resolution was executed by Rajesh Kumar with support from the incident
response team. After applying the remediation steps, the team monitored the
system for a stabilization period to confirm that the issue was fully
resolved and no secondary or cascading failures had occurred. All affected
services were verified to be operating within normal parameters before the
incident was formally closed.

========================================================================
8. PREVENTIVE ACTIONS
========================================================================

The following preventive actions have been identified to reduce the
likelihood and impact of similar incidents in the future:

  1. Implement query routing to automatically redirect analytical queries to read replicas
  2. Set innodb_lock_wait_timeout to 10 seconds with application retry logic
  3. Add long-running query detection with automatic kill after threshold
  4. Create network-level access control limiting reporting tools to read replicas
  5. Implement query classification and routing in the connection proxy

These action items will be tracked in the engineering backlog and prioritized
based on risk assessment and business impact analysis.
