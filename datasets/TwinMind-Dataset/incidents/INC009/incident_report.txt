1. SUMMARY
========================================================================

Internal DNS resolution for all service-to-service communication failed at 15:42 UTC on March 10, 2025. The Route 53 private hosted zone associated with the production VPC became unresponsive after an automated cleanup script accidentally deleted the hosted zone association. All microservices using DNS-based service discovery lost the ability to resolve internal hostnames, causing cascading failures across the entire platform.

This incident was classified as P1 severity and was assigned to
Maria Garcia in the Networking department. The affected system was
Route 53 Private Hosted Zone, specifically the internal-dns-resolver-01 component located in US-East-1 (AWS).
The incident was detected through automated monitoring and alerting systems,
and the response team was engaged within minutes of the initial alert.

========================================================================
2. BUSINESS IMPACT
========================================================================

Complete internal service mesh failure lasting 25 minutes. All inter-service communication ceased, affecting every customer-facing feature. The platform was effectively offline for all users globally. Approximately 68,000 users were impacted. Estimated revenue loss of $340,000.

7. RESOLUTION
========================================================================

The incident was resolved by the following actions: Re-associated the Route 53 private hosted zone with the production VPC, flushed DNS caches on all service nodes, and restarted affected services to clear stale DNS entries.

The resolution was executed by Maria Garcia with support from the incident
response team. After applying the remediation steps, the team monitored the
system for a stabilization period to confirm that the issue was fully
resolved and no secondary or cascading failures had occurred. All affected

========================================================================
8. PREVENTIVE ACTIONS
========================================================================

The following preventive actions have been identified to reduce the
likelihood and impact of similar incidents in the future:

  1. Add deletion protection on Route 53 hosted zone associations
  2. Implement dry-run mode for all infrastructure cleanup scripts
  3. Create DNS resolution canary monitoring with 30-second intervals
  4. Add IAM guardrails preventing hosted zone association deletion
  5. Implement service mesh fallback to IP-based routing during DNS failures

These action items will be tracked in the engineering backlog and prioritized
based on risk assessment and business impact analysis.
