1. SUMMARY
========================================================================

HAProxy reverse proxy (haproxy-prod-chi-01) began returning 503 Service Unavailable errors at 16:20 UTC on October 13, 2025. A maintenance automation script put all backend servers into drain mode simultaneously instead of one at a time for rolling updates. With all backends in drain state, HAProxy had no healthy servers to route requests to.

This incident was classified as P2 severity and was assigned to
Alex Johnson in the SRE department. The affected system was
HAProxy Load Balancer, specifically the haproxy-prod-chi-01 component located in On-Premises DC-Chicago.
The incident was detected through automated monitoring and alerting systems,
and the response team was engaged within minutes of the initial alert.

========================================================================
2. BUSINESS IMPACT

Complete service unavailability for on-premises application stack for 15 minutes. All 4,500 internal users were affected. Internal business applications, HR systems, and finance tools were inaccessible. Several time-sensitive business processes were disrupted.

7. RESOLUTION
========================================================================

The incident was resolved by the following actions: Manually re-enabled all backend servers in HAProxy, killed the errant maintenance script, and fixed the script to process servers sequentially with health verification between each.

The resolution was executed by Alex Johnson with support from the incident
response team. After applying the remediation steps, the team monitored the
system for a stabilization period to confirm that the issue was fully
resolved and no secondary or cascading failures had occurred. All affected
services were verified to be operating within normal parameters before the
incident was formally closed.

========================================================================
8. PREVENTIVE ACTIONS
========================================================================

The following preventive actions have been identified to reduce the
likelihood and impact of similar incidents in the future:

  1. Fix maintenance script to drain servers one at a time with health verification
  2. Implement minimum healthy backends guard preventing full pool drain
  3. Add HAProxy backend availability monitoring with zero-backend alerting
  4. Create maintenance window scheduling with change management approval
  5. Implement HAProxy drain protection requiring manual override for last healthy backend

These action items will be tracked in the engineering backlog and prioritized
based on risk assessment and business impact analysis.
