1. SUMMARY
========================================================================

HashiCorp Consul service discovery cluster (consul-prod-cluster-01) experienced a leader election failure at 12:40 UTC on October 6, 2025. The Consul cluster lost quorum when 2 of 3 server nodes experienced simultaneous garbage collection pauses exceeding the leader election timeout. Without a leader, service registration and health check updates stopped, causing stale routing information that directed traffic to deregistered service instances.

This incident was classified as P1 severity and was assigned to
Mike Torres in the Platform Engineering department. The affected system was
HashiCorp Consul, specifically the consul-prod-cluster-01 component located in US-East-1 (AWS).
The incident was detected through automated monitoring and alerting systems,
and the response team was engaged within minutes of the initial alert.

========================================================================
2. BUSINESS IMPACT
========================================================================

Service routing errors across all microservices for 20 minutes. Approximately 30% of inter-service requests were routed to non-existent endpoints. 22,000 users experienced intermittent errors. All service deployments were blocked during the outage. Estimated revenue impact of $180,000.

The incident triggered the organization's major incident response process,
involving cross-functional coordination between engineering, operations, and
business stakeholders. Customer support was notified and prepared to handle
incoming queries related to the service degradation.

6. ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
========================================================================

The root cause of this incident was determined to be:
Simultaneous JVM garbage collection pauses on 2 of 3 Consul server nodes exceeded the leader election timeout, causing quorum loss and service discovery failure.

Further analysis confirmed that this issue had been developing over time but
was not detected by existing monitoring thresholds and alerting rules. The

========================================================================
7. RESOLUTION
========================================================================

The incident was resolved by the following actions: Restarted Consul agents with tuned JVM garbage collection parameters, restored quorum, forced re-registration of all services, and expanded the cluster to 5 nodes for improved fault tolerance.

The resolution was executed by Mike Torres with support from the incident
response team. After applying the remediation steps, the team monitored the
system for a stabilization period to confirm that the issue was fully
resolved and no secondary or cascading failures had occurred. All affected
services were verified to be operating within normal parameters before the
incident was formally closed.

========================================================================
8. PREVENTIVE ACTIONS
========================================================================

The following preventive actions have been identified to reduce the
likelihood and impact of similar incidents in the future:

  1. Expand Consul cluster from 3 to 5 server nodes for better fault tolerance
  2. Tune JVM GC parameters on Consul servers to minimize pause times
  3. Increase leader election timeout to accommodate worst-case GC scenarios
  4. Implement Consul health monitoring with quorum loss detection
  5. Deploy Consul on dedicated instances without co-located workloads

These action items will be tracked in the engineering backlog and prioritized
based on risk assessment and business impact analysis.
