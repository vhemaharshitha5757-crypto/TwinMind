1. SUMMARY
========================================================================

A feature flag misconfiguration in LaunchDarkly at 16:45 UTC on June 9, 2025 accidentally enabled the new-checkout-v2 flag for 100% of users instead of the intended 5% canary group. The new checkout flow was incomplete, missing payment validation logic, and had known UI rendering issues. Users attempting to complete purchases through the new flow encountered errors and broken layouts.

This incident was classified as P2 severity and was assigned to
Priya Sharma in the Application Engineering department. The affected system was
LaunchDarkly Feature Flags, specifically the feature-flag-new-checkout-v2 component located in US-East-1 (AWS).
The incident was detected through automated monitoring and alerting systems,
and the response team was engaged within minutes of the initial alert.

========================================================================
2. BUSINESS IMPACT
========================================================================

All users globally were exposed to the unfinished checkout experience for 22 minutes. Checkout conversion rate dropped from 12% to 2%. Approximately 3,400 users encountered checkout errors. An estimated 1,200 abandoned carts resulted in $95,000 in potential lost revenue.

The incident triggered the organization's major incident response process,
involving cross-functional coordination between engineering, operations, and
business stakeholders. Customer support was notified and prepared to handle
incoming queries related to the service degradation.
