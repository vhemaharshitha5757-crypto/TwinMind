1. SUMMARY
========================================================================

Kafka consumer lag on the order-events topic spiked from near-zero to 2.4 million messages at 03:18 UTC on April 7, 2025. The consumer group order-processor experienced a rebalance storm triggered by one consumer instance hitting a GC pause longer than the session timeout. The repeated rebalances prevented consumers from making progress, causing the lag to grow at 15,000 messages per minute.

This incident was classified as P2 severity and was assigned to
Priya Sharma in the Data Engineering department. The affected system was
Apache Kafka Cluster, specifically the kafka-prod-broker-03 component located in US-East-1 (AWS).
The incident was detected through automated monitoring and alerting systems,
and the response team was engaged within minutes of the initial alert.

========================================================================
2. BUSINESS IMPACT
========================================================================

Order processing delayed by up to 45 minutes during the lag buildup period. Real-time order tracking became unreliable. Warehouse fulfillment systems received delayed picking instructions. Approximately 2,400 orders experienced delayed confirmation emails. No order data was lost.

The incident triggered the organization's major incident response process,
involving cross-functional coordination between engineering, operations, and
business stakeholders. Customer support was notified and prepared to handle
incoming queries related to the service degradation.

The following symptoms were observed during the incident:

========================================================================
6. ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
========================================================================

The root cause of this incident was determined to be:
Consumer group rebalance storm triggered by GC pauses exceeding the session timeout (30s) on one consumer instance, causing repeated group coordinator disruptions.

Further analysis confirmed that this issue had been developing over time but
was not detected by existing monitoring thresholds and alerting rules. The
failure point was within the kafka-prod-broker-03 component of the Apache Kafka Cluster
system. Contributing factors included insufficient monitoring coverage for
the specific failure mode encountered and the absence of automated
remediation procedures for this particular scenario.

========================================================================
7. RESOLUTION
========================================================================

The incident was resolved by the following actions: Increased consumer session timeout to 120 seconds, tuned JVM garbage collection parameters, added a dedicated consumer instance, and manually reset consumer offsets to drain the lag.

The resolution was executed by Priya Sharma with support from the incident
response team. After applying the remediation steps, the team monitored the
system for a stabilization period to confirm that the issue was fully
resolved and no secondary or cascading failures had occurred. All affected
services were verified to be operating within normal parameters before the
incident was formally closed.

========================================================================
8. PREVENTIVE ACTIONS
========================================================================

The following preventive actions have been identified to reduce the
likelihood and impact of similar incidents in the future:

  1. Tune JVM GC parameters for all Kafka consumer services
  2. Increase session timeout to accommodate worst-case GC pauses
  3. Implement consumer lag alerting at 100K and 500K message thresholds
  4. Add consumer group stability monitoring for rebalance frequency
  5. Deploy Kafka consumer auto-scaling based on lag metrics

These action items will be tracked in the engineering backlog and prioritized
based on risk assessment and business impact analysis.
