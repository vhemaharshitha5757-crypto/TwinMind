1. SUMMARY
========================================================================

A network partition between Availability Zones us-east-1a and us-east-1b occurred at 01:45 UTC on July 7, 2025. The partition isolated the database primary (in AZ-a) from its replicas and application servers (in AZ-b). The application's consensus layer detected the partition and promoted a replica in AZ-b to primary, resulting in a split-brain scenario with two active primaries writing conflicting data for 12 minutes before the partition healed.

This incident was classified as P1 severity and was assigned to
Maria Garcia in the Networking department. The affected system was
AWS VPC Networking, specifically the vpc-prod-us-east-1 component located in US-East-1 (AWS).
The incident was detected through automated monitoring and alerting systems,
and the response team was engaged within minutes of the initial alert.

Split-brain condition caused 847 conflicting database records across 3 tables. Data reconciliation required 6 hours of manual effort by the data engineering team. 4 customers received duplicate order confirmations. Financial records required audit. Estimated remediation cost of $35,000.

The root cause of this incident was determined to be:

Further analysis confirmed that this issue had been developing over time but

========================================================================
7. RESOLUTION
========================================================================

The incident was resolved by the following actions: Partition self-healed after 12 minutes, manual split-brain resolution performed by identifying and merging conflicting records, consensus algorithm updated to require majority quorum across all AZs.

The resolution was executed by Maria Garcia with support from the incident
response team. After applying the remediation steps, the team monitored the
system for a stabilization period to confirm that the issue was fully
resolved and no secondary or cascading failures had occurred. All affected
services were verified to be operating within normal parameters before the
incident was formally closed.

========================================================================
8. PREVENTIVE ACTIONS
========================================================================

The following preventive actions have been identified to reduce the
likelihood and impact of similar incidents in the future:

  1. Update consensus algorithm to require strict majority quorum across AZs
  2. Implement fencing tokens to prevent split-brain write conflicts
  3. Add network partition detection with automatic write-lock on minority partition
  4. Create data reconciliation runbook for split-brain scenarios
  5. Deploy cross-AZ network monitoring with partition detection alerts

These action items will be tracked in the engineering backlog and prioritized
based on risk assessment and business impact analysis.
