1. SUMMARY
========================================================================

The webhook delivery service (webhook-dispatcher-prod-01) began experiencing widespread delivery timeouts at 11:30 UTC on October 27, 2025. A major partner's webhook receiver endpoint slowed down from 200ms to 30-second response times, and the webhook dispatcher's thread pool was blocked waiting for responses from this single partner. Since the dispatcher used a shared thread pool, the slow partner's webhooks blocked delivery to all other partners.

This incident was classified as P3 severity and was assigned to
Jenny Liu in the API Engineering department. The affected system was
Webhook Delivery Service, specifically the webhook-dispatcher-prod-01 component located in US-West-2 (AWS).
The incident was detected through automated monitoring and alerting systems,
and the response team was engaged within minutes of the initial alert.

========================================================================
2. BUSINESS IMPACT
========================================================================

Webhook delivery delays of 45 minutes for all 120 webhook subscribers. 580,000 webhook events queued up during the incident. Partners relying on real-time webhooks experienced stale data. 15 partners opened support tickets about delayed webhooks.

The incident triggered the organization's major incident response process,
involving cross-functional coordination between engineering, operations, and
business stakeholders. Customer support was notified and prepared to handle
incoming queries related to the service degradation.

========================================================================
7. RESOLUTION
========================================================================

The incident was resolved by the following actions: Implemented per-partner thread pool isolation with individual timeout settings, added circuit breaker for slow endpoints, and drained the queued events with increased parallelism.

The resolution was executed by Jenny Liu with support from the incident
response team. After applying the remediation steps, the team monitored the
system for a stabilization period to confirm that the issue was fully
resolved and no secondary or cascading failures had occurred. All affected
services were verified to be operating within normal parameters before the
incident was formally closed.

========================================================================
8. PREVENTIVE ACTIONS
========================================================================

The following preventive actions have been identified to reduce the
likelihood and impact of similar incidents in the future:

  1. Implement per-partner thread pool isolation (bulkhead pattern)
  2. Add circuit breaker per webhook subscriber with configurable thresholds
  3. Set aggressive connection and read timeouts for webhook delivery (5s max)
  4. Implement webhook delivery priority queues for critical partners
  5. Add partner endpoint health monitoring with automatic suspension

These action items will be tracked in the engineering backlog and prioritized
based on risk assessment and business impact analysis.
