1. SUMMARY
========================================================================

MySQL read replica mysql-replica-eu-02 developed a replication lag of 45 minutes at 17:45 UTC on August 25, 2025. A large batch UPDATE operation on the primary (updating 8 million rows) was replicated as row-based events, which the replica processed sequentially. Application read queries routed to this replica returned stale data, causing users to see outdated order statuses and inventory counts.

This incident was classified as P2 severity and was assigned to
Rajesh Kumar in the Database Engineering department. The affected system was
MySQL Replication Cluster, specifically the mysql-replica-eu-02 component located in EU-West-1 (AWS).
The incident was detected through automated monitoring and alerting systems,
and the response team was engaged within minutes of the initial alert.

========================================================================
2. BUSINESS IMPACT
========================================================================

EU users seeing stale data for 90 minutes during replication catch-up. Approximately 5,400 users saw incorrect order status or inventory availability. 45 orders placed based on stale inventory data required manual fulfillment adjustment. Customer trust impacted.

The incident triggered the organization's major incident response process,
involving cross-functional coordination between engineering, operations, and
business stakeholders. Customer support was notified and prepared to handle
incoming queries related to the service degradation.

5. INVESTIGATION
========================================================================

Upon receiving the automated alert, the incident response team initiated a
systematic investigation following established runbook procedures. The team

Log analysis from the affected component showed error patterns that pointed

records, configuration modifications, and deployment history to rule out

========================================================================
6. ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
========================================================================

The root cause of this incident was determined to be:
Large batch UPDATE on 8 million rows replicated as row-based binlog events, causing the single-threaded SQL applier on the replica to fall 45 minutes behind the primary.

Further analysis confirmed that this issue had been developing over time but
was not detected by existing monitoring thresholds and alerting rules. The
failure point was within the mysql-replica-eu-02 component of the MySQL Replication Cluster
system. Contributing factors included insufficient monitoring coverage for
the specific failure mode encountered and the absence of automated
remediation procedures for this particular scenario.

========================================================================
7. RESOLUTION
========================================================================

The incident was resolved by the following actions: Temporarily routed EU read traffic to the primary, enabled parallel replication on the replica, chunked the batch operation for future use, and waited for replica to catch up.

The resolution was executed by Rajesh Kumar with support from the incident
response team. After applying the remediation steps, the team monitored the
system for a stabilization period to confirm that the issue was fully
resolved and no secondary or cascading failures had occurred. All affected
services were verified to be operating within normal parameters before the
incident was formally closed.

========================================================================
8. PREVENTIVE ACTIONS
========================================================================

The following preventive actions have been identified to reduce the
likelihood and impact of similar incidents in the future:

  1. Enable multi-threaded parallel replication on all read replicas
  2. Implement batch operation chunking policy limiting to 100K rows per transaction
  3. Add replication lag monitoring with alerts at 5-minute and 15-minute thresholds
  4. Create read routing policy to exclude replicas with lag above threshold
  5. Implement automated read traffic failover when replication lag is detected

These action items will be tracked in the engineering backlog and prioritized
based on risk assessment and business impact analysis.
