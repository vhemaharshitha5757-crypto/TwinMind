1. SUMMARY
========================================================================

Amazon SES SMTP relay service (ses-smtp-prod-01) stopped delivering outgoing emails at 06:00 UTC on November 3, 2025. The SES sending quota was exhausted after a marketing batch email job accidentally sent emails to the entire customer database (2.1 million addresses) instead of the intended 50,000-person segment. The sending quota was consumed in 2 hours, blocking all subsequent transactional email delivery.

This incident was classified as P2 severity and was assigned to
Priya Sharma in the Application Engineering department. The affected system was
Amazon SES SMTP Relay, specifically the ses-smtp-prod-01 component located in US-East-1 (AWS).
The incident was detected through automated monitoring and alerting systems,
and the response team was engaged within minutes of the initial alert.

========================================================================
2. BUSINESS IMPACT
========================================================================

All outgoing email notifications blocked for 3 hours until quota reset. Transactional emails including password resets, order confirmations, shipping notifications, and two-factor authentication codes were not delivered. Approximately 45,000 transactional emails queued. 2,800 users unable to complete password resets or 2FA login.

The incident triggered the organization's major incident response process,
involving cross-functional coordination between engineering, operations, and
business stakeholders. Customer support was notified and prepared to handle
incoming queries related to the service degradation.

========================================================================

Further analysis confirmed that this issue had been developing over time but

========================================================================
7. RESOLUTION
========================================================================

The incident was resolved by the following actions: Requested emergency SES quota increase from AWS, prioritized transactional email queue over marketing, implemented separate SES configurations for transactional vs marketing email.

The resolution was executed by Priya Sharma with support from the incident
response team. After applying the remediation steps, the team monitored the
system for a stabilization period to confirm that the issue was fully
resolved and no secondary or cascading failures had occurred. All affected
services were verified to be operating within normal parameters before the
incident was formally closed.

========================================================================
8. PREVENTIVE ACTIONS
========================================================================

The following preventive actions have been identified to reduce the
likelihood and impact of similar incidents in the future:

  1. Implement separate SES accounts for transactional and marketing email
  2. Add segment size validation check before batch email sends
  3. Set sending rate limits on marketing email pipeline
  4. Implement SES quota usage monitoring with alerts at 70% and 90%
  5. Create batch email approval workflow requiring segment verification

These action items will be tracked in the engineering backlog and prioritized
based on risk assessment and business impact analysis.
