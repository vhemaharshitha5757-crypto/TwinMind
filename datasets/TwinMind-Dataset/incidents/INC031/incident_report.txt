1. SUMMARY
========================================================================

An IAM policy update at 13:00 UTC on August 11, 2025 as part of a least-privilege hardening initiative inadvertently removed permissions required by the CI/CD deployment pipeline. The updated policy removed s3:PutObject and ecr:PushImage permissions from the deployment role, blocking all production deployments. The team discovered the issue when three critical hotfix deployments failed consecutively.

This incident was classified as P2 severity and was assigned to
David Kim in the Security Engineering department. The affected system was
AWS IAM, specifically the iam-deploy-role-prod component located in US-East-1 (AWS).
The incident was detected through automated monitoring and alerting systems,
and the response team was engaged within minutes of the initial alert.

========================================================================
2. BUSINESS IMPACT
========================================================================

All production deployments blocked for 1.5 hours. Three critical security hotfixes were delayed. Development team velocity halted as feature branches could not be deployed. No direct customer impact but security patching window was missed.

The incident triggered the organization's major incident response process,
involving cross-functional coordination between engineering, operations, and
business stakeholders. Customer support was notified and prepared to handle
incoming queries related to the service degradation.

========================================================================
8. PREVENTIVE ACTIONS
========================================================================

The following preventive actions have been identified to reduce the
likelihood and impact of similar incidents in the future:

  1. Implement IAM policy simulation testing before applying permission changes
  2. Create IAM permission dependency map for CI/CD pipelines
  3. Use AWS Access Analyzer to validate policy changes against actual usage
  4. Implement staged IAM policy rollout with canary testing
  5. Add IAM policy change approval workflow with deployment team review

These action items will be tracked in the engineering backlog and prioritized
based on risk assessment and business impact analysis.
