1. SUMMARY
========================================================================

A single failure in the inventory-svc-prod microservice triggered a cascade failure across the entire order processing pipeline at 13:22 UTC on May 5, 2025. The inventory service became unresponsive due to a database connection pool leak, and downstream services lacked circuit breakers. Each service in the chain held connections open waiting for inventory service responses, exhausting their own connection pools and thread pools, propagating the failure upstream to the API gateway.

This incident was classified as P1 severity and was assigned to
James Wright in the Backend Engineering department. The affected system was
Microservice Mesh, specifically the inventory-svc-prod component located in US-East-1 (AWS).
The incident was detected through automated monitoring and alerting systems,
and the response team was engaged within minutes of the initial alert.

========================================================================
2. BUSINESS IMPACT
========================================================================

Complete order pipeline outage for 42 minutes. All new orders, order modifications, and order status queries failed. Approximately 28,000 users were unable to complete purchases. Estimated revenue loss of $450,000. Cart abandonment rate spiked to 95%.

The incident triggered the organization's major incident response process,
involving cross-functional coordination between engineering, operations, and
business stakeholders. Customer support was notified and prepared to handle
incoming queries related to the service degradation.

8. PREVENTIVE ACTIONS
========================================================================

The following preventive actions have been identified to reduce the
likelihood and impact of similar incidents in the future:

  1. Implement circuit breakers on all inter-service communication boundaries
  2. Add connection pool monitoring and leak detection for all services
  3. Deploy service mesh with automatic retry and timeout policies
  4. Create chaos engineering program to test cascade failure scenarios
  5. Implement bulkhead pattern to isolate critical service dependencies

These action items will be tracked in the engineering backlog and prioritized
based on risk assessment and business impact analysis.
