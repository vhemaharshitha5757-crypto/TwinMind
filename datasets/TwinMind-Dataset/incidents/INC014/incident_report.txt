========================================================================
1. SUMMARY
========================================================================

The RabbitMQ production cluster (rabbitmq-prod-eu-01) in the EU region experienced a queue overflow event at 22:10 UTC on April 14, 2025. The notification-events queue grew beyond its configured memory limit of 4GB, triggering RabbitMQ's flow control mechanism. However, the publisher did not implement backpressure handling and continued publishing, eventually causing the broker to drop messages when disk space was also exhausted. An estimated 34,000 notification messages were permanently lost.

This incident was classified as P1 severity and was assigned to
Mike Torres in the Platform Engineering department. The affected system was
RabbitMQ Cluster, specifically the rabbitmq-prod-eu-01 component located in EU-West-1 (AWS).
The incident was detected through automated monitoring and alerting systems,
and the response team was engaged within minutes of the initial alert.

========================================================================
2. BUSINESS IMPACT
========================================================================

34,000 customer notification messages (email and SMS) were permanently lost. Customers did not receive order confirmation, shipping, and payment notifications for a 2-hour window. Customer support received 1,800 inquiries about missing notifications. Manual notification resend was required for affected customers.

The incident triggered the organization's major incident response process,
involving cross-functional coordination between engineering, operations, and
business stakeholders. Customer support was notified and prepared to handle
incoming queries related to the service degradation.

6. ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
========================================================================

The root cause of this incident was determined to be:
Publisher service did not implement RabbitMQ backpressure handling, continuing to publish messages during flow control, leading to message drops when both memory and disk limits were breached.

Further analysis confirmed that this issue had been developing over time but
was not detected by existing monitoring thresholds and alerting rules. The

========================================================================
7. RESOLUTION
========================================================================

The incident was resolved by the following actions: Cleared the overflowed queue, implemented publisher-side backpressure handling with confirms, increased disk space allocation, and resent lost notifications from application event log.

The resolution was executed by Mike Torres with support from the incident
response team. After applying the remediation steps, the team monitored the
system for a stabilization period to confirm that the issue was fully
resolved and no secondary or cascading failures had occurred. All affected
services were verified to be operating within normal parameters before the
incident was formally closed.

========================================================================
8. PREVENTIVE ACTIONS
========================================================================

The following preventive actions have been identified to reduce the
likelihood and impact of similar incidents in the future:

  1. Implement mandatory publisher confirms and backpressure handling
  2. Add queue depth monitoring with alerts at 1GB and 3GB
  3. Configure dead letter queues for overflow scenarios
  4. Implement message persistence for critical notification events
  5. Create disaster recovery playbook for message loss scenarios

These action items will be tracked in the engineering backlog and prioritized
based on risk assessment and business impact analysis.
